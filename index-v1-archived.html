03 · The age of AI

How buyers find businesses has changed, and what that means for you

For two decades, being found meant ranking on Google's blue links. That still matters, but it is no longer the whole picture. Google now answers many questions directly with an AI Overview at the top of the page, so a buyer can read a recommendation without clicking a single website. At the same time, a fast-growing share of people skip the search box entirely and ask ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude or Perplexity to recommend a provider, compare options or explain what to look for. These assistants reply with a short list of specific businesses, and if your company is not among the sources they trust, you are invisible at the exact moment a buyer is deciding.

The reassuring part is that you do not need a separate, mysterious strategy to win here. AI systems draw on the same evidence that earns a Google ranking: pages they can crawl, clear answers near the top, strong and consistent business facts, schema that spells out what you do, real reviews, and mentions from trusted websites. A business that has done its SEO well is already most of the way to being cited by AI. The rest of the work is about structure and clarity. We make each answer easy to quote. We keep your business details (name, services, locations, credentials) the same everywhere. And we make sure the facts an AI repeats about you are the ones you would choose.

In practical terms, we treat the two fronts as one programme. The page that answers a buyer's question clearly enough to rank on Google is usually the same page an AI Overview pulls its summary from, so we write and structure once for both. We keep your business facts, name, services, areas served and credentials, identical across the website, the Google Business Profile and the directories, because an assistant that finds conflicting details will hesitate to recommend you. And we add the schema and clear, near-the-top answers that make a page easy to quote. The effect is that as buyers shift from typing into Google to asking an assistant, your visibility moves with them and stays strong.

What this means in practice is that getting found now has two fronts, and the businesses that prepare for both pull ahead while competitors are still arguing about keyword rankings alone. It also raises the stakes on trust: an AI assistant will only recommend a business it can verify, so consistent details, genuine reviews and a clear, well-structured website matter more than ever. We build for both fronts from the start, classic search and AI search together, so the work you invest in being found keeps working as more buyers move to AI, and it holds its value as the channel matures. That is what "getting found in the age of AI" actually means: one set of strong foundations, working across every place a buyer now looks.

05 · How our SEO works

The work behind better rankings and more enquiries

Here is what our SEO covers at a glance, the detail lives on the SEO services page. Each piece is sequenced around the work most likely to create enquiries first.

It starts with keyword research and strategy: a commercial map of the searches your buyers make, grouped by intent and weighed by how likely each is to create an enquiry. Competitor analysis shows which terms rivals already rank for and which gaps you can win, so we build the high-value pages first; a vast content calendar can wait. Then comes technical SEO, which makes sure Google and AI systems can crawl, render and index the site cleanly: indexation, site speed and Core Web Vitals, clean URL structure, internal links, structured data and redirects. The best content cannot rank if search engines cannot reach it, so the technical foundation usually comes first and often produces the quickest early wins.

On-page SEO and content are where rankings and enquiries are won together. We give each page a precise title and meta description, a logical heading structure, internal links to the pages that matter, schema, and copy written to help the buyer decide, around the questions they ask. Around the money pages we build supporting guides and comparison content, so the site covers a topic with real depth, which is also what earns featured snippets and AI citations. If you serve an area, local SEO is often the fastest route to enquiries. We improve the Google Business Profile. We keep your name, address and phone the same everywhere. We earn and reply to reviews, build local listings, and write real location pages. That helps you show up in the map pack.

In competitive markets, authority decides who wins. So link building and digital PR earn mentions and links from real publications and local organisations. We aim for links a human editor would choose to give. Those keep their value as algorithms change. Search now spans more than Google, so AI search work happens at the same time. We make your business easy for ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews to understand and cite. It uses the same clear answers, clean details, schema and trusted mentions that help a page rank. Done together, these pieces add up. Every useful page, technical fix and earned mention keeps working.

On-page SEO
Changes on your own site: page titles, headings, content, internal links, and speed.
Off-page SEO
Trust earned from other sites, mainly links and mentions from places people respect.

08 · Cost & timeline

What it costs, and when results show

UK SEO pricing depends on competition, how many pages need work, technical debt and how fast you want to move. A small local campaign needs less production than a competitive national or ecommerce campaign, so it costs less; a business that wants results quickly invests more in the early months. We scope the first phase around the work most likely to create enquiries, keep it month to month so you can stop any time, and show what each pound of budget is buying. If paid ads or local work would create enquiries faster while SEO builds, we will say so plainly.

On timing, early movement often appears within a few weeks once technical fixes and local improvements land. Stronger enquiry growth usually builds over three to six months, while competitive national terms take longer because they need sustained authority. We attack the achievable terms first and approach the hardest ones from a position of strength, and the monthly report shows progress long before the headline rankings move, so you can always see what is happening.

It also helps to see how your budget sets the pace of the work. A smaller monthly budget concentrates on one or two workstreams at a time, say technical fixes and the highest-value pages, so progress is real but steady. A larger budget runs more in parallel: content, authority and local work together, which compresses the timeline and reaches competitive terms sooner. Neither is better in the abstract; the right level depends on how fast you want enquiries to grow and how competitive your market is. Because the agreement is monthly, you can start at a level that feels comfortable and increase it once the early work has proved the return, and the larger spend waits until you have seen results. We are explicit about what each level of investment buys, so the pace is always your decision to make with full information.
